# LFO for Kick + Bass Combination

## Key Insights

### 1. Kick vs Bass Leadership
- **One must lead** - Can't have both at full power
- Bass leads → kick shorter/punchier
- Kick leads → bass ducks around it

### 2. Sub Mono Rule
- Keep sub (below ~100Hz) mono
- Width only on higher bass layers
- Wide sub disappears on real systems

### 3. Frequency Separation
- Create "lanes" - don't stack traffic
- Kick dominates one range
- Bass dominates another
- Remove low freq from other sounds

### 4. Groove Over Volume
- Quiet low end with groove > loud without
- Let silence create movement
- Align bass rhythm with kick

### 5. Sidechain = Tool Not Crutch
- Don't overuse sidechain
- Focus on timing first
- Light sidechain > heavy processing

### 6. Shorten Low-End
- Tighten envelopes
- Reduce overlap time
- Low end should feel tight, not long

### 7. LFO Application
- LFO on filter for bass movement
- LFO on kick pitch for variation
- Sync LFO to kick pattern

## LFO Ideas for Kick+Bass Combo
- **Kick filter:** LFO on lowpass for "whoosh"
- **Bass filter:** LFO for rhythmic pumping
- **Parallel:** Two LFO rates (slow movement + fast groove)
