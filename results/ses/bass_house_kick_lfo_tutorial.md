# Bass House Kick with LFO - Tutorial

## Bass House Kick Characteristics

- **Four-on-the-floor** - Classic 4/4 beat, 125-128 BPM
- **Punchy** - Strong attack/transient
- **Sub presence** - Not as deep as techno, more mid-forward
- **Groove-oriented** - Works with the bass rhythm

## LFO Usage on Bass House Kicks

### 1. Pitch Envelope (Essential)
```
Start: 120-150Hz → End: 40-50Hz
Decay: 50-100ms
```
- Quick pitch drop = punch
- Slower = more "rounded" feel

### 2. LFO for "Wobble" (Optional)
- Rate: 2-4 Hz (subtle)
- Depth: 5-10% (barely noticeable)
- Shape: Sine

### 3. Filter LFO (Rare in Bass House)
- Bass house kick usually stays "dry"
- If used: lowpass at 200Hz, very subtle

## Step-by-Step: Bass House Kick

### Step 1: Basic Tone
- Oscillator: Sine + slight noise
- Start at 100Hz, pitch envelope to 45Hz
- Short decay (80ms)

### Step 2: Add Attack
- Click/transient layer (3-5ms)
- Boost at 2-4kHz for "snap"

### Step 3: Sub Body
- Add second oscillator one octave lower
- Keep subtle (-6dB from main)

### Step 4: LFO (Optional)
- Only if you want "wobble" variation
- 4Hz, depth 5%, sine wave

### Step 5: Glue
- Light compression (2:1, 10ms attack, 100ms release)
- Master at -6dB

## LFO Settings Summary

| Style | Pitch Drop | LFO Rate | LFO Depth |
|-------|------------|----------|-----------|
| Classic | 100Hz→45Hz | - | - |
| Wobble | 100Hz→45Hz | 2-4 Hz | 5-10% |
| Deep | 80Hz→35Hz | - | - |

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too much sub** - Bass house needs mid-punch
2. **No attack** - Missing transient = no "punch"
3. **Over-LFO'd** - Bass house kicks are usually clean
