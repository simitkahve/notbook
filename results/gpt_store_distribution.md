# GPT Store Distribution Guide

Research summary on getting featured and promoted in OpenAI's GPT Store.

---

## 1. How to Create and Publish a GPT

### Steps to Create:
1. Open ChatGPT and click "Create a GPT"
2. Define the GPT's behavior, capabilities, and instructions in the Builder
3. Configure knowledge (upload files) and capabilities (browsing, DALL-E image generation, code interpreter)
4. Add conversation starters to help users get started

### Steps to Publish:
1. Open your GPT in the editor
2. Select **Share**
3. Choose **GPT Store** as the sharing level
4. Select a category for your GPT
5. Review how your builder name/website appears
6. Confirm the GPT meets policy requirements
7. Publish

### Important Notes:
- You must be a **ChatGPT Plus or Pro subscriber** to publish to the public GPT Store
- Changes after publishing are saved to a draft first — select **Update** to apply them to the live GPT

---

## 2. Requirements for GPT Store Listing

### Eligibility:
- **ChatGPT Plus/Pro subscription** required
- Must complete your **Builder Profile** (name, optional website from verified domain, optional social profiles)
- For GPTs using **Actions**: Each public action must include a valid **Privacy Policy URL**

### Publishing Constraints:
A GPT may NOT be eligible if:
- Public publishing is disabled for your workspace
- The GPT uses "apps" (plugins)
- The GPT uses actions without a valid Privacy Policy URL
- The GPT fails sharing or policy checks
- Your workspace/account has restrictions preventing marketplace publishing

### Policy Requirements:
- Public GPTs must meet OpenAI's policy and product requirements
- If publishing is blocked, you can update the GPT and try again, or appeal the decision

## 3. How to Get Featured/Trending

### Official Factors (from OpenAI):
The GPT Store highlights **featured** and **trending** GPTs from community and partner creators. Key ranking factors include:

- **Engagement**: More user interactions = higher visibility
- **Ratings & Reviews**: Positive reviews enhance credibility and ranking
- **ChatGPT Signups**: Referrals that lead to new signups improve ranking
- **Keyword Relevance**: Using popular, relevant keywords in name and description
- **Uniqueness**: Distinctive features that stand out

### GPT Store Optimization (GSO) Strategies:

1. **Name Optimization**:
   - Use distinctive, memorable names
   - Include target keywords users likely search for
   - Research top-ranking GPTs and extract common keywords

2. **Description Optimization**:
   - Write clear, compelling descriptions with secondary keywords
   - Help users understand capabilities at a glance

3. **Logo Design**:
   - Create simple, recognizable logos
   - Use keyword-rich image filenames for SEO

4. **Marketing & Promotion**:
   - Include website links in your profile
   - Cross-promote multiple GPTs via smart links
   - Encourage users to leave reviews after positive interactions
   - Promote on social media, blogs, and online platforms
   - Build quality backlinks to your GPT
   - Update your GPT regularly to show active maintenance

5. **Performance Monitoring**:
   - Track analytics to identify what works
   - Use data-driven approach for optimization

## 4. Success Stories & Top GPT Examples

### Featured GPTs (from OpenAI launch):
- **AllTrails** — Trail recommendations
- **Khan Academy** — Learning assistant
- **Canva** — Design assistant

### High-Performing Categories:
Based on store data, popular GPTs tend to focus on:
- **Productivity** (writing, research, summarization)
- **Education** (tutoring, learning aids)
- **Creative** (design, image generation assistance)
- **Programming** (code help, developer tools)
- **Lifestyle** (travel, health, fitness)

### Key Insight:
Over **3 million custom GPTs** have been created since the feature launched. With ~159K public/active GPTs in the store, differentiation and quality are critical for visibility.
